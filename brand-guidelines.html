2. Target Audience Primary Customer Demographics: Adults (approx. 25-60) in the Calgary area, interested in professional skincare, anti-aging, and wellness treatments. Typically tech-savvy and values quality and expertise. Define who the brand serves and how to communicate with them effectively.
Psychographics & Motivations:
  • Values self-care and invests in high-quality, professional treatments.
  • Seeks visible, lasting results and is willing to follow expert advice for aftercare.
  • Motivated by a desire to address specific skin concerns (e.g., acne scars, signs of aging, pigmentation) and enhance their overall appearance and confidence.
  • Appreciates a luxurious, comfortable, and private spa experience.
Online Behavior:
  • Actively researches specific treatments (e.g., "microneedling," "laser hair removal") on Google before booking.
  • Follows local beauty/wellness influencers and brands on Instagram and TikTok.
  • Relies heavily on before/after photos, client testimonials, and detailed FAQs to make decisions.
  • Likely to search for localized keywords, such as "HydraFacial downtown Calgary."

10. Content Strategy Content Pillars/Themes:
  • Educational: Explain the science, benefits, and process of each treatment. Detail pre-care and aftercare instructions.
  • Results-Oriented: Showcase before & after galleries. Share client testimonials and success stories.
  • Expertise-Driven: "Meet the Aestheticians" features, explain the technology used, and break down complex topics in an accessible way.
  • Brand Experience: Highlight the luxurious and comfortable spa environment. Offer a "behind-the-scenes" look at the locations.
 Guide content creation for marketing channels.

6. Brand Voice Tone: Professional, reassuring, results-focused, welcoming, and expert. It should be clinical but accessible. Ensure consistent communication and brand personality.
Brand Personality Archetype: A blend of The Sage (trusted expert, knowledgeable) and The Caregiver (nurturing, reassuring, supportive).
Example phrases that fit/don't fit the brand:
  • Fit: "Transform Your Beauty Journey", "Premier Aesthetic Treatments", "Look and feel your absolute best".
  • Don't Fit: Overly casual slang, unscientific language, high-pressure sales tactics ("Act now! Limited time!").
Tagline (if exists): "Premier Aesthetic Treatments in Calgary"
7. Customer Experience Key touchpoints: Website (service pages, pricing/booking, contact), two physical spa locations, social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok), email, and phone. Align interactions with brand values.
How customers should feel when interacting with the brand: Cared for, confident, relaxed, and assured that they are in expert hands.
8. Marketing & Campaign Guidelines Primary Call-to-Action (CTA): "Book Appointment" / "Book Now" Guide the execution of marketing campaigns and promotions.
Secondary Call-to-Action (CTA): "View Services" / "Learn More"
Promotional Tone: Promotions should be framed as valuable opportunities (e.g., "Seasonal Rejuvenation Package," "New Client Offer") rather than discounts. The language must remain premium and aligned with the brand's expert positioning. Avoid terms like "cheap," "sale," or "clearance."
